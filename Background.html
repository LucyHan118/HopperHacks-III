The History of Plastic

Plastic was first invented in 1869 by John Wesley Hyatt, and was first mass produced by Leo Baekeland in 1907 to save nature materials. For the first time human manufacturing was not constrained by the limits of nature. The creation of new materials also helped free people from the social and economic constraints imposed by the scarcity of natural resources. Inexpensive celluloid made material wealth more widespread and obtainable. However, things took a sudden twist down the road, beginning in the 1960s, which is a decade in which Americans became increasingly aware of environmental problems, the environment became heavily polluted by plastic materials.
