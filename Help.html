How Can I Help?

Dispose Plastic Safely


The best way to not contribute to plastic waste is to avoid buying plastic objects in the first place. Of course, that’s not always possible so we come to our second best way, and most practical; a sanitary landfill. The plastic is flattened so it takes up the minimum of space. In a way, plastic does well to keep petroleum out of the atmosphere.



On a more individual level, you can recycle plastic or reuse it. Plastic can be remelted and reshaped to other products. Contrary to popular belief, plastic bags and wrapping can be reconstituted back into oil. Since plastic seems to be here to stay, we must use and create more environmentally conscious ways to dispose of it.

What NOT To Do With Plastic


1) Microwave or pour hot liquid into a plastic container


2) Discard all plastic waste in recycling bins


3)Think that buying plastic is cheaper


4) Use disposable plastic because it’s more convenient


5) Leave cigarettes out on the ground

In fact, cigarette butts — whose filters contain tiny plastic fibers — are the most common type of plastic waste found in the environment in a recent global survey. Drink bottles, bottle caps, food wrappers, grocery bags, drink lids, straws and stirrers were the next most common items.
